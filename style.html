BIT 3434: Advanced Modeling for Business Analytics

In this class, we learned a lot about using models and analytics to solve problems in businesses. We used Excel spreadsheets to solve a lot of these problems and to make the models. We also learned about non-linear programming, heuristics, and other methods. Our final project was a team project in which we made a website about genetic algorithms, a problem-solving method based on natural selection, and how it is applied in business.

BIT 3464: Enterprise Planning and Resource Control

In this class, we learned a lot about how to use different ERP systems in businesses. We learned about processes in business, such as materials requirement planning, CRM, and production planning. We also learned how to use the SAP software. For our final project and report, we toured the company MOOG, learned all about its production procceses, and gave a presentation to the class detailing our findings.

BIT 4474: Global Operations and Information Technology

In this class, we learned about how businesses operate globally and how they globalize their information technology services. We learned about trading systems across the world global communication, international information systems, and more. We learned about partnerships between the USA, Singapore, Japan, China, and a multitude of other countries. For our final project, my group and I created a Wix website that was a business primer for a hypothetical global consulting firm in Mexico, and we discussed the would-be business culture, logistics and trading, business ethics, and more.

FIN 3104: Introduction to Finance

This was an introductory finance class, focusing on various topics from the time value of money to risk and return to investment to capitalization. We created a stock portfolio on a simulator on Investopedia, and experimented with the stock market.

BIT 4484: Project Management

In this class, we learned a lot about project management tools and techniques, such as Gantt charts, communications, stakeholder analyses, risks, cost management, scope statements, and more. The primary part of this class was to run our own project. My group's project was called "Hokies for the Humane Society." We worked with the Humane Society of Montgomery County, an animal shelter in Christiansburg, VA. We raised funds and awareness through percentage night events, and we raised supplies through two very successful supply drives. Our project turned out to be a greak success!
